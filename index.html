Many cars are limited by onboard charger (OBC) power. AC destination charging matches how guests actually stay—slow, safe, and practical.

Why destination AC charging wins (for hotels + guests)

These are the exact advantages you were highlighting—structured and easy to understand.

Guest Fit: overnight is the natural charging window

  • Guests stay for hours—charging can happen quietly in the background.
  • No waiting line stress like highway DC fast chargers.
  • Perfect for tourism / pilgrimage endpoints (guests arrive, park, rest).

Hotel Fit: low operations, high amenity value

  • Charging becomes an amenity (like Wi-Fi) — not a complex infrastructure project.
  • Lower site upgrade needs vs DC fast charging.
  • Cleaner experience at parking: less cable mess, fewer complaints.

Grid + Safety: lower current, lower stress

  • Lower peak demand reduces the risk of tripping / overloading.
  • Safer thermal profile for cabling and distribution.
  • Better for hotels where power is shared across HVAC, kitchens, lifts, etc.

Vehicle Reality: the car decides the AC rate (OBC limit)

  • If a car’s onboard charger is 3.3 kW, it won’t draw 7.4/11/22 kW even if the charger supports it.
  • That’s why AC destination charging works well as a standard offering.
  • Fast charging can be added later when utilization justifies it.
